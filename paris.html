Dreaming of Paris

Trip Overview

Paris feels like a city full of art, history, and beautiful views, so I imagine spending several days walking around, visiting museums, sitting in cafés, and exploring different neighborhoods. I want to see the famous landmarks, but also enjoy slow mornings, street markets, and smaller cultural spots that make the city feel unique.

Landmarks

The Eiffel Tower, the Louvre, Notre-Dame, and the Arc de Triomphe are classic stops. I’d love to see them, but also find smaller plazas and quiet viewpoints that aren’t crowded with tourists.

Cafés & Bakeries

Coffee with a view of the street, fresh baguettes, croissants, and desserts lined up behind glass spending a slow morning in a Parisian café is high on my list.

Neighborhood Walks

From Montmartre to the Latin Quarter, I want to wander through neighborhoods to see everyday life: balconies, flower boxes, small shops, and winding streets.

Evening Views

In the evenings, I imagine walking along the Seine, listening to street music, and watching the city lights reflect on the water and bridges.

If I ever make it there, I want simple days walking along the river, reading on a bench, trying pastries, and watching life unfold from café windows.

Longer-Term Travel Notes

I don’t have firm plans to visit Paris yet, but I’m slowly gathering ideas. As I learn more about transportation, seasons, and neighborhoods, I’ll add packing lists, walking routes, and sample itineraries here.

For now, this section is a place for rough notes, half-formed plans, and inspiration enough text to keep the page full and readable while I keep researching and dreaming about a future trip.
